Emergency Preparedness Guides

1. Create an Emergency Plan

  • Communication: Establish a communication plan with family members, including emergency contacts and meeting places.
  • Evacuation Routes: Know the nearest evacuation routes and shelters. Practice the routes regularly with your family.
  • First Aid Knowledge: Ensure at least one family member knows basic first aid and CPR.

2. Prepare an Emergency Kit

  • Water: Store at least 1 gallon of water per person per day for at least three days.
  • Food: Stock up on non-perishable food like canned goods, energy bars, and freeze-dried meals.
  • First Aid Supplies: Include bandages, antiseptic wipes, and over-the-counter medications.
  • Flashlight & Batteries: Ensure you have a working flashlight with extra batteries.
  • Radio: Have a battery-powered or hand-crank radio for emergency updates.
  • Personal Items: Include personal hygiene items like soap, sanitary products, and wet wipes.

3. Stay Informed

  • Weather Alerts: Use apps or websites to stay updated on weather conditions.
  • Local Authorities: Follow local government and emergency services on social media for real-time alerts.
  • Emergency Alerts: Sign up for emergency alerts in your area for immediate notifications about disasters.

4. Protect Your Home

  • Secure Heavy Objects: Anchor heavy furniture and appliances to prevent them from falling during earthquakes or storms.
  • Window Protection: Use storm shutters or impact-resistant windows to protect against high winds or flying debris.
  • Fire Extinguisher: Keep a fire extinguisher in key areas like the kitchen.
  • Backup Power: Consider investing in a generator to power essential devices during power outages.

5. Practice Emergency Drills

  • Fire Drill: Practice fire drills with your family and ensure everyone knows how to exit the house safely.
  • Earthquake Drill: Practice "Drop, Cover, and Hold On" drills in case of an earthquake.
  • Flood Drill: Know where to go during floods (higher ground or evacuation center).

6. Prepare for Specific Emergencies

  • Floods: Elevate electrical appliances above potential flood levels, especially in flood-prone areas.
  • Tornadoes: Identify a safe room or basement and keep emergency supplies there.
  • Hurricanes: Install storm shutters and clear any debris around the house.
  • Wildfires: Clear vegetation around your property and have an evacuation plan in place.

7. Health and Medical Preparedness

  • Medications: Keep a 7-10 day supply of prescription medications in your emergency kit.
  • Special Needs: Plan for infants, elderly members, or people with disabilities and ensure they have necessary supplies and transportation.
  • Health Insurance Information: Keep a copy of your health insurance card and any important medical documents.

8. Stay Calm and Be Ready

  • Mental Preparedness: Mentally prepare for an emergency by practicing calmness and thinking clearly under pressure.
  • Community Connections: Stay connected with neighbors to support one another during emergencies.
  • Stay Flexible: Be ready to adapt your plan as new information or situations arise.
